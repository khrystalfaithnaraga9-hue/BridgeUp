Why Poverty Still Matters Today

Poverty isn’t just a number—it’s the everyday barrier that keeps people from finishing school, getting decent work, and accessing healthcare. It affects food on the table, time for learning, and the ability to recover from shocks like price spikes or disasters. In the Philippines, official statistics showed 15.5% of Filipinos were living below the poverty line in 2023 (Philippine Statistics Authority).

Beyond official counts, perception data helps us see how families feel their situation on the ground. In nationwide Social Weather Stations (SWS) surveys, many families continue to rate themselves as poor, with especially high levels in Visayas and Mindanao. What’s Being Done Right Now

The Philippine government’s flagship anti-poverty program, the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps), provides conditional cash transfers to low-income households to keep children in school and improve health and nutrition. It was institutionalized through RA 11310, recognizing it as a national poverty reduction strategy. Source: DSWD

Beyond cash assistance, agencies and LGUs run livelihood, scholarship, feeding, and skills-training initiatives, while civil society groups support food security, community kitchens, and learning recovery programs. These efforts help—but gaps remain in access, scale, and sustainability, especially in rural and disaster-prone areas.
